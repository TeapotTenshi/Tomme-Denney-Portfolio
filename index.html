About Me

I’m an XR developer with a background in Game Design and a passion for crafting immersive, meaningful experiences across virtual and mixed reality. I'm currently pursuing a Master of Science in Extended Reality (XR) with a concentration in A.I. at Northeastern University.

My work spans across VR, AR, and MR — from controller-free hand interaction on Meta Quest 3 to AI-driven locomotion research in VR environments. Whether prototyping narrative-driven experiences or optimizing performance for low-spec hardware, I approach every challenge with a balance of creativity and technical precision.

I’ve contributed to XR projects in academic, professional, and hackathon settings, with a strong emphasis on accessibility, interaction design, and player immersion. Certified through XR Terra and trained in ethical research practices, I’m deeply interested in how immersive media can shape human experience, storytelling, and education.

Projects

An inclusive XR art tool built with haptic feedback and multisensory interaction to support visually impaired creators.
View Project on Devpost

This project was born out of a desire to redefine artistic expression for visually impaired individuals, utilizing the principles of cognitive extension through touch, tactile communication, and multisensory integration. During the 2025 MIT Reality Hackathon, we aimed to create a tool that not only empowers users to create but also enables them to connect with their work in a deeply immersive and accessible way. By incorporating haptic feedback and spatial mapping, we sought to bridge the gap between art and accessibility, ensuring inclusivity for all creators.
